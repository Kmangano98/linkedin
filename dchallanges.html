Autoimmune Disease Machine Learning Challenge

Organized by the Eric and Wendy Schmidt Center at the Broad Institute (USA), this challenge spanned three parts and brought together thousands of participants to address complex biomedical prediction tasks using deep learning.

  • Part 1 (Oct 2024 ‚Äì Feb 2025): ü•à 2nd Place / 75 submissions / 7,800 participants ‚Äì $2,400 prize

Overview: Autoimmune diseases such as IBD affect millions globally. The challenge aimed to develop models that can infer costly transcriptomic data from accessible inputs like pathology images. Each sub-challenge focused on a different aspect of transcriptome prediction and gene prioritization.

  • Task 1: Predict RNA expression profiles from pathology images using ResNet50 (end-to-end deep learning).
  • Task 2: Use predicted partial gene expression to infer full-cell transcriptomes with a neural network.
  • Task 3: Rank genes based on their ability to distinguish normal vs. dysplastic cells using AUC and feature importance methods (e.g. Integrated Gradients).

These projects were implemented in Python, PyTorch and run on Google Colab.
